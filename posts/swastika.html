The Truth about the Swastika

Summary of Findings

Wikipedia’s Swastika article foregrounds the Nazi appropriation, labeling it “the most recognisable modern use” and placing Hitler’s hooked-cross design front and center, which risks overshadowing the symbol’s millennia-old auspicious role in Hinduism and related traditions. In contrast, Vedic literature consistently portrays the svastika (su + asti + ka) as a mark of well-being, prosperity, and cosmic order. Below, we first expose Wikipedia’s editorial agenda, then set forth the exact Sanskrit shlokas and their translations to reaffirm the swastika’s original meaning.

1. Exposing Wikipedia’s Agenda

1.1 Lead Framing and Imagery

  • The very first caption on Wikipedia’s Swastika page calls the Nazi use “the most recognisable modern use” of the symbol, equating it with its ancient spiritual form.
  • The lead paragraph defines the swastika predominantly through its association with the German Nazi Party, skewing public perception before its sacred origins are presented.

1.2 Category and Structural Emphasis

  • The article is filed equally under “Symbols of Nazi Germany” and “Hindu symbols,” placing Nazi misuse on par with religious heritage.
  • Section headings like “Nazism” appear much earlier in the table of contents than detailed treatments of Hinduism, Buddhism, or Jainism, suggesting a bias.

2. True Vedic Meaning of the Swastika

2.1 Etymology in Sanskrit

The Sanskrit word svastika (स्वस्तिक) comes from su (good, well) + asti (is, exists) + -ka, meaning “that which is conducive to well-being.” It stands for good fortune, health, and auspiciousness across Vedic texts.

2.2 Rig Vedic Invocation

“With well-being (svasti) we would follow along our path, like the Sun and the Moon...”

This invocation frames svasti as cosmic well-being, guiding humanity with celestial forces.

2.3 Vedic Śānti Mantra

ॐ भद्रं कर्णेभिः शृणुयाम देवाः ।
भद्रं पश्येमाक्षभिर्यजत्राः ।
स्थिरैरङ्गैस्तुष्टुवागँसस्तनूभिः ।
वयशेम देवहितं यदायूः ।
स्वस्ति न इन्द्रो वृद्धश्रवाः ।
स्वस्ति नः पूषा विश्ववेदाः ।
स्वस्ति नस्ताक्षर्यो अरिष्टनेमिः ।
स्वस्ति नो बृहस्पतिर्दधातु ।
ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥

This peace invocation blesses health, longevity, protection, and prosperity—underscoring that svasti, the root of svastika, means “auspicious blessing” in every domain of life.
